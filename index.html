About Me

I began learning web development at the end of 2021. In 2022, I joined a remote bootcamp that simulated the structure and demands of a real-world job. For five days a week, eight hours a day, I worked on projects under a structured schedule. Each day started with a team meeting where we often rotated leadership roles, ensuring tasks were completed on time. Working closely with developers from around the world, I built projects using pair programming (driver-navigator method) and organized group work with the Kanban method. I also frequently worked independently, developing strong time management skills.

Over the past year, I’ve worked on freelance projects and, in early 2024, launched a YouTube channel to teach HTML and CSS fundamentals to new developers. I also completed the #100DaysOfCode challenge, creating front-end components and websites daily and sharing my progress on the channel. This experience highlighted my consistency and dedication to achieving my goals.
